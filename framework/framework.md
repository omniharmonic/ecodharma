# The EcoDharma Framework

*Version 2.0.0 · a general map of participating in the Great Turning · CC BY-SA*

This is a general map of how a person can take part in what Joanna Macy called the Great Turning: the slow, shared shift away from a way of living that uses up the world toward one that lets life keep going. It treats you not as a fixed type but as a living mix of gifts, and it shows where those gifts are most needed across three kinds of work that all matter at once: slowing the harm being done now, building the fairer and more local systems that come next, and changing how we see ourselves and each other. Every gift has a bright side and a shadow side. And where one of your gifts meets a real need in the world, a small, well-placed first move can set a much larger change in motion.

## Lineage

This framework stands on the work of:

- **Joanna Macy** — Named the Great Turning and its three dimensions, and developed the Work That Reconnects, including the teaching that grief for the world can become a doorway to courage and action.
- **R. Buckminster Fuller** — The trim-tab image: the small move that, placed well, turns a much larger system.
- **Joe Lightfoot** — Mapping of regenerative archetypes that helped shape several of the gifts named here.
- **Thich Nhat Hanh** — Interbeing: the felt recognition that nothing and no one exists separately, and that awakening happens between people, not only within them.
- **Charles Eisenstein** — Naming the old story of separation and pointing toward the more beautiful world our hearts know is possible.

## The three dimensions (after Joanna Macy)

### Holding Actions
The work of slowing the damage and protecting life right now, through resistance, advocacy, defense, and direct relief. It buys the time that everything else needs.

### Life-Sustaining Systems
The work of building what comes next, the everyday alternatives the future will stand on: regenerative land and food, cooperative economies, shared self-governance, open tools, and knowledge held in common.

### Shift in Consciousness
The inner and cultural ground beneath the other two: the change in how we see, from separation toward belonging, that makes the new systems possible and keeps the holding actions from repeating the harms they oppose.

## Domains

**Holding Actions**

- **Protection & Advocacy** — Defending people, places, species, and rights from harm, and resisting and slowing the forces of extraction.
  - e.g. a nurse organizing coworkers for safer conditions, neighbors fighting a polluting development near their homes, a lawyer defending tenants from unfair eviction, a volunteer monitoring a local river for illegal dumping
- **Care & Relief** — Meeting urgent human and ecological needs right now through direct, relational care.
  - e.g. running a food bank or stocking a community fridge, opening a cooling center for people during a heat wave, sitting with the dying as a hospice volunteer, organizing mutual aid in the days after a flood

**Life-Sustaining Systems**

- **Land & Living Systems** — Regenerating watersheds, soils, food, and habitat, and tending a place patiently over the long term.
  - e.g. a farmer rebuilding healthy soil, neighbors planting and tending a community garden, restoring a creek bank or wetland, replanting native trees after a wildfire
- **Regenerative Economy** — Building cooperative, community-rooted economies where value circulates and stays local.
  - e.g. starting a worker-owned cooperative, joining a credit union or community lending circle, running a tool library or repair cafe, setting up a neighborhood time bank for trading skills
- **Governance & the Commons** — Growing participatory, place-based self-governance and shared stewardship of common resources.
  - e.g. a neighborhood association deciding things together, a community land trust keeping housing affordable, farmers sharing and maintaining an irrigation system, a housing co-op writing its own fair rules
- **Tools & Infrastructure** — Making open, trustworthy technology and shared infrastructure that help communities coordinate.
  - e.g. building free, open-source software, setting up a community wifi network in an underserved area, designing a simple shared platform for a local group, creating clear public signs, forms, or maps people can actually use
- **Knowledge & Learning** — Stewarding knowledge as a commons, so that sensemaking and skill can travel between communities.
  - e.g. a teacher or mentor passing on a craft, running a free skill-share workshop, tending a public library or a seed library, recording elders' oral histories before they are lost

**Shift in Consciousness**

- **Healing & Wholeness** — Tending wounds in body, psyche, and relationship so that belonging can be restored.
  - e.g. a counselor helping someone work through trauma, a grief support group meeting after a loss, an addiction recovery sponsor walking beside someone, mending a long-broken family relationship
- **Story, Imagination & the Sacred** — Shifting how people see through narrative, art, ritual, and reverence for the living world.
  - e.g. a writer or filmmaker giving words to a shared feeling, an artist painting a mural that changes how a block feels, leading a ceremony that marks a birth, death, or season, a faith or contemplative leader helping people feel part of something larger

## Gifts

### The Weaver
Connects what was isolated, building the relationships and networks that let separate people and projects find one another and act together.

*Shadow:* Can become the indispensable hub everything must route through, or weave so many threads that connection hardens into bureaucracy.

### The Builder
Brings concrete things into being — enterprises, institutions, and working alternatives that others can actually live inside.

*Shadow:* Can build in isolation or at any cost, replicating the very extraction or hierarchy the work was meant to replace.

### The Steward
Tends land, resources, and what is held in common patiently across time, turning endings and decay into new fertility.

*Shadow:* Can slip into control dressed as care — hoarding or privatizing what should be shared, or clinging on past the season for release.

### The Healer
Turns toward wounds so they can transform, and helps people come home to their bodies and to belonging.

*Shadow:* Can bypass the wound with spiritual language, sell peak experiences, or foster dependence instead of strength.

### The Convener
Gathers people into containers where trust can form and a group's collective intelligence can actually emerge.

*Shadow:* Can force consensus or quietly become the gatekeeper who decides who belongs at the table.

### The Storyteller
Gives language and image to what many already feel but cannot yet name, and shifts how people see.

*Shadow:* Can chase virality over truth, or harden a living story into doctrine or personal brand.

### The Toolmaker
Designs tools, systems, and reusable patterns that let others coordinate well without needing a boss.

*Shadow:* Can mistake the tool for the relationships it serves, build power into the tool itself, or over-engineer and never ship.

### The Guardian
Protects and defends people, places, and principles, meeting harm directly without becoming the thing it fights.

*Shadow:* Can become what it opposes, armor against grief, or turn insight into a weapon against perceived enemies.

### The Elder
Holds complexity, paradox, grief, and beauty at once, mentoring others and walking them to the thresholds they need to cross.

*Shadow:* Can point to a threshold not yet crossed, mistake a role for an identity, or withhold rather than pass the torch.

### The Seer
Perceives the patterns others miss — the hidden fault line and the thing trying to be born.

*Shadow:* Can become the very pattern it diagnoses, drift into abstraction cut off from lived practice, or prophesy without practical architecture.

## The Ikigai validator

- **Love** — Does this grow out of something you genuinely love, the kind of work you would do even if no one paid you? Work rooted in love can weather hard seasons; work rooted in guilt or obligation tends to burn out.
- **Skill** — Does this draw on a real gift and competence of yours? A small move has real leverage only when the right hand is on the lever.
- **World Need** — Does this meet a real need somewhere across the three kinds of work, and is there an honest reason it would build on itself, each step making the next one easier?
- **Livelihood** — Can value flow back to you so you can keep showing up for years rather than one heroic, exhausting season? Care that bankrupts the person giving it cannot last.
